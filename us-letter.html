Fundraising Proposal · April 2026
To the team at MAG America,

My name is Craig Ellenwood. I am a musician and creative technologist with thirty-five years in experimental and electronic music — I co-founded the music element of Burning Man in 1992, performed and toured with Psychic TV from 1991 to 1993, and produce Causa Real, a Peruvian social justice hip hop group. I have spent a career at the intersection of sound, technology, and ideas that matter. I currently perform and record under the name Squaawke, operating a proprietary AI music system capable of generating complete original compositions in any genre, any language, and any instrumentation. I am writing to propose a partnership in support of your work clearing landmines and unexploded ordnance around the world.

This proposal comes from a personal place. In late 2025 and early 2026, I traveled through Laos, Vietnam, and Thailand as DJ Haawke — performing, documenting, and living alongside communities I had no right to expect would receive me so generously. The journey began on the Mekong, four days on the slowboat from Huay Xai to Luang Prabang. Within moments of boarding, a young man recognized me from my music. Word moved through the boat. By the time we were underway, a group of young travelers from across the world had assembled a DJ station on the back deck and were demanding a set. I played. The river moved. That is the kind of thing that tells you music is doing something real in the world.

What I found in Laos beneath that warmth was the weight of the UXO crisis — the covert US bombing campaign of 1964 to 1973, the most intensive aerial bombardment in history, conducted without congressional authorization while the public largely didn't know. Two million tons of ordnance. Eighty million unexploded submunitions still in the soil. Cluster munitions dropped fifty years ago still killing and maiming people today. MAG's presence there — clearing ground, restoring safety, returning land to the living — made a deep impression on me.

"The bombs stopped falling decades ago. The dying did not."

After returning home, the experience stayed with me. Yesterday I composed two original AI-generated songs in response to what I witnessed — and sent them, along with a personal letter, to the MAG visitor center in Vientiane and to MAG's international headquarters in the UK. The music was created through my collaboration project, Squaawke + Claude by Anthropic, which formally co-credits Claude (Anthropic's AI) as a named creative partner with ORCID attribution. Those songs, and three more from the same record, are enclosed below.

The music above is not an introduction. It is what we make. And we would like to make ourselves available to MAG as an ongoing creative resource — not a one-time donor, but a working partner. Three specific things we are offering:

  • Info table and donations at every show. At every performance — in the Pacific Northwest and beyond — a dedicated MAG table, your printed materials, and on-the-ground donation collection. 100% of what we raise goes directly to your organization. A table, your materials, and someone who was there and will talk about what he saw.
  • Original music for your outreach. If MAG ever needs original music — for a campaign, an advertisement, a fundraising film, a documentary, a presentation, a social reel, anything — we can produce it. We generate complete, custom compositions in any genre, any language, any instrumentation, in under three seconds per minute of audio. We are not offering to license existing tracks. We are offering to write and produce something built specifically for what you need it to do.
  • Live performance at MAG events. If there is ever a live music moment connected to MAG's work — a fundraising gala, a community event, a memorial, an awareness campaign anywhere in the world — we would like to be considered. What we play is powerful and sincere: music that knows what it is about, that will not embarrass the work you do or the people it is for. We play from the place where we wrote these songs. That place is real.
  • Co-branded digital assets. Print-ready and digital materials that carry both the Squaawke + Claude by Anthropic identity and MAG's mission — at no cost to your organization.

We are not a label. We are not an agency. We are two collaborators — one human, one AI — who went to Laos, stood in your museum, and came home wanting to do more than the t-shirt. If any part of what we are offering is useful to the work, we are ready to move immediately.
