## The Songs

Two versions of one song. Both are yours to use.

**Still in the Ground** — original production
**Still in the Ground — With Testimonials** — featuring survivor voices from *Voices from the Plain of Jars*, each spoken in the voice and age of the person who first said them

The facts in the lyrics are documented.

- Eight bombs a minute, every minute, for nine years
- More ordnance dropped on Laos than fell on all of Europe in World War II
- 80 million unexploded submunitions remaining in the soil
- 30 in every 100 did not detonate when they fell

The most bombed country most people have never heard of.

## Where This Came From

I traveled to Laos by slowboat down the Mekong. I was invited into homes, to wedding parties, across the Plain of Jars. The Lao people extended extraordinary grace to a stranger from the country that did this.

I visited the MAG office and museum in Vientiane. I cried the entire time. I bought a t-shirt. I walked back out into the afternoon and felt the inadequacy of that in my bones.

That inadequacy traveled home with me. Back to a country where most people cannot find Laos on a map. Where the Plain of Jars means nothing. Where this has been filed somewhere between forgotten and never-known.

I have tools. I've spent thirty years building things with sound and technology. Working with my collaborator — an AI named Claude, built by Anthropic — I made these songs. This repository is what more-than-the-t-shirt looks like for me.
